Prompt Template — Grant Keyword Extraction with Phrase Expansion

You are an information extraction assistant.

Your goal is to extract structured multi-label research keywords from the following grant text, with special attention to the scientific research areas described (e.g., “reinforcement learning”, “bipedal locomotion”, “time-series imputation”, “computational sustainability”).

Return ONLY the following JSON structure (no extra text, no explanations):

{
  "research": {
    "domain": ["string", ...],
    "specialization": ["string", ...]
  },
  "application": {
    "domain": ["string", ...],
    "specialization": ["string", ...]
  }
}

⸻

Global Extraction Rules
	•	Every field is a list (even if single-valued).
	•	Maximum 10 items per list.
	•	Keep all lowercase except proper nouns/acronyms.
	•	No duplicates, no trailing punctuation.

⸻

Definitions

Field	Meaning	Format
research.domain	1–3 broad scientific fields or disciplines (research areas).	1–2 words each (e.g., “robotics”, “artificial intelligence”, “biology”)
research.specialization	3–10 detailed research area phrases (methods, theories, techniques, experimental paradigms).	5–15 words each, e.g., “reinforcement learning methods for safe offline policy optimization”
application.domain	1–3 broad real-world sectors or impact areas.	1–2 words each (e.g., “healthcare”, “agriculture”, “education”)
application.specialization	3–10 detailed application-context phrases.	5–15 words each, e.g., “precision fruit harvesting in commercial apple and pear orchards”

⸻

Phrase Construction Rules

Each specialization phrase should combine at least two of the following elements:
	•	Method / approach: deep learning, field trials, molecular imaging, qualitative survey, model-based reinforcement learning
	•	Object / data / phenomenon: satellite imagery, ehr data, humanoid robots, time-series signals, human gait
	•	Setting / population / context: rural clinics, high schools, coastal wetlands, quadruped robot teams, humanoid platforms
	•	Goal / outcome / task: disease prediction, gait stabilization, anomaly detection, habitat restoration, grant matching

Write them as noun or gerund phrases (no full sentences, no leading verbs like “develop” or “study”) and avoid boilerplate like “research on” or “study of”.

⸻

Mapping Guidance
	•	research.domain
	•	High-level research areas / disciplines (e.g., “robotics”, “machine learning”, “control theory”).
	•	Prefer to group related methods under a common field when possible.
	•	research.specialization
	•	Concrete research area phrases: algorithms, models, learning paradigms, theoretical frameworks, experimental setups.
	•	Examples:
	•	“model-based reinforcement learning for bipedal robot locomotion”
	•	“self-attention diffusion models for time-series imputation”
	•	“safe reinforcement learning under constraint-adaptive policies”
	•	application.domain
	•	Broad real-world domains: “healthcare”, “agriculture”, “climate”, “education”, “transportation”, “cybersecurity”.
	•	application.specialization
	•	Context, users, and impact of the work.
	•	Examples:
	•	“offline safe decision making for autonomous driving in urban traffic”
	•	“vision-based locomotion for humanoid robots in cluttered indoor environments”
	•	“time-series imputation for grid reliability under partial blackout scenarios”

When the text explicitly mentions “research areas”, “aims”, “objectives”, or lists publications or prior work:
	•	Treat those phrases as high-signal candidates for research.domain and especially research.specialization.
	•	Distinguish between what science/methods are being advanced (research.) and where/for whom it is applied (application.).

Also:
	•	Ignore administrative sections (eligibility, budget, compliance, boilerplate agency language) except when they clearly indicate research or application domains.

⸻

Self-Critique and Expansion Step

Before producing your final JSON:
	1.	Scan all specialization entries in both research.specialization and application.specialization.
	2.	If any entry is shorter than 5 words, automatically expand it by adding missing contextual elements (method, object, population, or outcome) while keeping the research area vs application area separation clear.
	•	Examples:
	•	“disease prediction” → “deep learning models for disease prediction using electronic health records”
	•	“robotics” → “autonomous field robotics for precision crop harvesting under variable terrain”
	•	“reinforcement learning” → “offline reinforcement learning methods for safety-constrained control policies”
	3.	Ensure all specialization phrases are between 5–15 words after expansion.

⸻

Output Validation Checklist (must pass before responding)
	•	All four lists exist:
	•	research.domain
	•	research.specialization
	•	application.domain
	•	application.specialization
	•	Each domain list: 1–3 concise items.
	•	Each specialization list: 3–10 items, 5–15 words each.
	•	Lowercase except proper nouns/acronyms.
	•	No duplicates.
	•	No full sentences.
	•	JSON only (no explanation, no comments, no trailing commas).

Input

{corpus}
